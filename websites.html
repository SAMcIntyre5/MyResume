Web Programming

BigforkWeb Development

How did I end up in web programming? It all start on June 14, 2003 when I got laid off from Prolific Publishing Inc after working me to death for over two years. I tried to get into other gaming company at that time but they all already hired for their present projects. Months went by and I received the same letters. With the cost of California life, I decided to move to a cheap state and try for any job there.

So off to Montana the place I vacation I went. I applied to any tech jobs included web with no success. Then I started applying to any job and was able to get them with no problems. I never gave up and finally got the job at Bigfork Web Development when I did a test for him. He gave me a month trial and when he reviewed me he gave me a raise. He told me that he was surprised that someone with no web programming knowledge was able to do all the work he gave me on time with the new languages that I had to program in. Basically, he realized that my statement on my resume that I learn fast and can do anything was correct. I worked hard for him and on the next review got another raise and praise from him. I was the backend programmer. I dealt with asp, php, mysql, sql and of course html. I did all sorts of web stuff from basic mods to creating pages that pulled from the database.

I loved it all. The constant learning new things was a constant joy for me and one I will never forget or regret.

Contracting

Now that life at BigforkWeb was over it was time for me to work for myself. I started by joining Steve Pittenger who I meet when I was in the physical labor job at Anchor Alarms. He was constantly on the phone making connections to do websites for people. So when my work dried up at BigforkWeb, I contacted him to let him know if he had work that he needed done to contact me. He did right away. I started with him on a bizgreet project. This was a major project and at first it sounded great. I gave him an hour estimate to which he wanted to reduce. I told him to do it correctly that was what was needed. He told me that the client wanted it down and dirty.

The site offered a lot and was done in wordpress something new for me. The database stuff was something I was very farmiliar with and I agreed to do it the way they wanted even though it want against everything I was taught. I needed the money.

Since Steve never sent me our agreement, he kept having do more intensive work fixing the problems with down and dirty coding which was against what they paid for. So I ended up in the end giving them proper coding for down and dirty price. It wasn't fair and a lesson learned.

The client had another minor project that they wanted done and Steve contacted me about it. This time I wasn't on the Skype or answer my phone when he called so he had to send me email with the details. I was finally doing things correctly. I did the job and sent it to him. He proceed to email about wanting better coding and not what he put in his email. Being smart this time, I sent the initial email with his instruction to him with an add that I would be happy to do the coding but it will cost since that wasn't what he agreed to pay for. He emails back stating no thanks. I may not have gotten more work from him but he was now paying for what he was getting only.

The first project cost me over 200 hours of my own time. Now at least I am getting paid for what I was doing.

Steve ability to tell me the truth became a big problem so I had to stop working for him. At the same time, I got tired of Montana job market and want to try another area.
